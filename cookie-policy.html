Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and tracking technologies on our website

Last updated: March 3, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how LaySun ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website (laysun.co). It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy, which provides further information about how we handle personal data collected through these technologies.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the website to remember information about your visit, such as your preferences or browsing activity. Cookies and similar technologies (including pixels, web beacons, and local storage) help us make our website work correctly and understand how visitors use it.

2. What Cookies and Tracking Technologies We Use

Our website uses two categories of cookies and tracking tools:

2.1 Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are required for the website to function properly. Without these, certain features may not work as intended. These cookies do not collect personal information and are not used for marketing or analytics. Examples include:

Cookie / Technology Purpose Duration
Session cookies Maintain website state and navigation functionality during your visit Session (deleted when browser is closed)
Preference cookies Remember user preferences such as menu state Session or short-term persistent

Essential cookies are set by our website directly (first-party cookies) and are necessary for the site to operate. They do not require your consent under most privacy regulations.

2.2 Analytics Cookies — Apollo.io Website Tracker

We use the Apollo.io website tracker to collect analytics data about how visitors interact with our website. This tool helps us understand visitor behavior, identify which pages and content are most relevant to our target customers, and support our B2B sales and marketing activities.

Tool Provider App ID Purpose
Apollo.io Website Tracker Apollo.io, Inc. 699435a6d52bb100156127ec Visitor analytics, page view tracking, and B2B visitor identification (company-level) using IP intelligence and third-party data

The Apollo.io tracker may set cookies or use local storage to track visitor sessions and page activity. It may also use your IP address in combination with third-party business data sources to identify the organization you belong to. This information is used by our sales team to understand which companies are visiting our website and to inform our outreach activities.

Apollo.io operates as a data processor on our behalf. For full details on how Apollo.io handles data, please refer to their Privacy Policy.

3. Cookies We Do Not Use

We want to be transparent about what we do not use:

  • Advertising or retargeting cookies: We do not currently use any advertising cookies, retargeting pixels, or third-party ad networks on our website. No advertising cookies are set or used.
  • Social media tracking pixels: We do not embed social media tracking pixels (e.g., Meta/Facebook Pixel, TikTok Pixel) on our website.

4. Third-Party Services That May Set Cookies

In addition to cookies set directly by LaySun, certain third-party services embedded in our website may set their own cookies:

Service Purpose Privacy Policy
Google Fonts Delivers web fonts (Inter, Playfair Display). Google may log requests but does not use this for advertising targeting. Google Privacy Policy
Apollo.io Website visitor analytics and B2B lead identification Apollo.io Privacy Policy

5. How to Control Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Block all cookies or block cookies from specific websites
  • Set preferences for first-party versus third-party cookies

Instructions for managing cookies in common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

5.2 Opting Out of Apollo.io Tracking

If you wish to opt out of tracking by Apollo.io, you can do so through their opt-out mechanism. Visit Apollo.io's privacy page for opt-out instructions. You may also use browser-level tracking protection features or privacy-focused browser extensions to block the Apollo.io tracker script.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers transmit a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal to websites. Our website does not currently respond to DNT signals in a standardized way, as there is no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to these signals. We will update this policy if a standard is established and we implement a response.

7. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time as our use of tracking technologies changes or as regulatory requirements evolve. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will reflect the most recent revision. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
