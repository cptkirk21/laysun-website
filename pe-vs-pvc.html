PE vs PVC: Why Material Matters in Artificial Plants

Not all artificial plant materials are equal. Here's the definitive guide to why PE (polyethylene) is the commercial standard — and what to look for when evaluating suppliers.

Manufacturing · 9 Feb 2025 · 9 min read

What is PE (Polyethylene)?

PE is an injection-molded plastic that creates highly realistic artificial plant leaves with detailed veining, natural textures, and realistic color variations.

What is PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)?

PVC is a cheaper plastic typically used in lower-quality artificial plants, characterized by flat, shiny surfaces and less realistic appearance.

Key Differences

PE offers superior realism, durability, and UV resistance compared to PVC, making it the preferred choice for commercial installations.

Commercial Applications

PE artificial plants are designed for long-term commercial use with better fire safety ratings and longer lifespan in high-traffic environments.

Supplier Evaluation

Always ask suppliers about material composition and request samples to verify PE construction for commercial projects.
